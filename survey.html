Survey Experiment: Retail vs Institutional Information

Design

Trade X is designed to mimic the information environment of a typical retail investor (delayed quotes, less granular data). Trade Y gives the same participants richer, almost real-time information, approximating an institutional setting. For each trade, respondents report:

  • Expected 1-week return for the company
  • Confidence in their decision (0–10)
  • Fraction of a \$10,000 account they would allocate
  • Loss amount that would make them equally unhappy as a \$100 gain
  • Perceived fairness of their information access (1–7)
