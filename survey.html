\ Survey Design\

Retail vs. Institutional: Trade X and Trade Y

\

\ We fielded an online survey of UChicago students who actively invest or\ follow financial markets. Respondents answered demographic questions,\ reported their investing experience and platforms, and then faced\ two stylized trading scenarios:\

\
    \
  • Trade X: Limited, delayed information \'96 designed to mimic a typical retail setup.
    • \
  • Trade Y: Rich, real-time information and analytics \'96 designed to mimic an institutional desk.
    • \
\

\ For each trade, respondents reported:\

\
    \
  • Expected 1-week return (%)
    • \
  • Self-reported confidence (0\'9610)
    • \
  • Fraction of a \\$10,000 account allocated
    • \
  • Perceived fairness of their information access (1\'967)
    • \
\

\ \

\ Paired t-tests compare each respondent\'92s Trade X and Trade Y answers:\

\
    \
  • Expected return: no statistically significant difference.
    • \
  • Confidence: significantly higher in Trade Y (p < 0.05).
    • \
  • Allocation: no strong evidence of a systematic change.
    • \
\

\ Interpretation: richer, institutional-style information made people feel\ more confident, but did not reliably move their expected returns.\ This suggests a risk that better tools amplify perceived edge\ more than true edge.\
