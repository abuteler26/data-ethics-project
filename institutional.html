Institutional Component

Historical Order-Flow & Institutional Comparison

This section summarizes the empirical analysis based on the historical Robinhood order-flow dataset (2018–2020) and institutional holdings data (using quaterly 13F filings from SEC). It provides real-world evidence of how retail investors behaved around major volatility spikes and compares this with institutional-level information access.

Data & Methodology

Methods:

  • Focused specifically on the following stocks: TSLA, AMCX, PLUG, JPM, JNJ, PG, XOM, BRK.B (and .A), AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, NVDA, GOOGL, MRNA, BA, F
  • Used earliest available 2018 data through the end of 2020 (including the pandemic period for most tickers).
  • Note that not all stocks have data covering the entire period, which is why some trend lines appear or disappear mid-series.

Key Insights

  • Empirical Evidence
    Retail order-flow showed strong sensitivity to price momentum, often reacting after
  • Institutional Advantage
    High-frequency and deeper data access allowed institutional investors to position ahead of volatile swings.
  • Survey Link
    The behavioral patterns seen in the dataset mirror the tendencies observed in Trade X survey responses.
