How Data Accessibility Shapes Retail vs. Institutional Investors

Project Abstract

We study how differences in information access affect decision-making, perceived fairness, and outcomes during high-volatility events.

Our project combines two sources of evidence:

  • A modern survey experiment where UChicago students make hypothetical trading decisions under two information environments: a retail-like setting (Trade X) with delayed / limited data, and an institutional-like setting (Trade Y) with richer, real-time information.
  • A historical Robinhood order-flow dataset that captures how actual retail investors behaved around extreme volatility between 2018–2020.

Ultimately, we ask: “How does unequal access to real-time, high-quality data affect retail vs. institutional investors’ decision-making and performance during volatility spikes, and what ethical concerns arise for fairness, responsibility, and transparency in financial markets?”
